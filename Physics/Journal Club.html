Background

What is a quantum computer?

Qubits, coupled together and controlled by gates, to do computation.

What is a qubit?

A two-level quantum system. Think spin up/down of an electron.

Transmon qubits

Josephson junction (barrier sandwiched between superconductors) + capacitor

Transmon qubit Hamiltonian

\(\hat{H}_{Tr} = 4E_C \hat{n}^2 - E_J \cos \hat{\varphi}\)

where
  • \(\hat{n}\) is the number of Cooper pairs traversed across the junction (charge operator)
  • \(\hat{\varphi}\) is the superconducting phase difference across the junction
  • \(E_J\), \(E_C\) are Josephson and charging energies

Transmon qubit as a quantum pendulum

Consider \(\hat{\varphi}\) as an angular variable with \(\hat{L_Z}=\hbar\hat{n}\), then:
  • \(E_C=\frac{\hbar^2}{8ml^2}\)
  • \(g=\frac{E_J}{ml}\)


Therefore:
\(\hat{H}_{Tr} = \frac{\hat{L_Z}^2}{2ml^2}-mgl\cos\hat{\varphi}\)

Coupling transmon qubits

Neighbours \(i\) and \(j\) are capacitively coupled (\(\hat{n_i}\hat{n_j}\), coupling strength \(T\)), giving the Hamiltonian for array of transmons:
\(\hat{H} = \sum_i \hat{H_{Tr}} + T\sum_{\langle i,j \rangle} \hat{n_i}\hat{n_j}\).

Qubit Array \(\leftrightarrow\) Coupled Pendula

What is a gate?

A manipulation of qubit state(s). For example, the classical NOT gate takes a classical bit and flips it from 0->1 or from 1->0.
